Capstone Problem Framing

Problem Statement

How Might We

How might India use generative and agentic AI to accelerate electronics capability building from design to field deployment?

Target State

An India-based electronics ecosystem where design, packaging/test, manufacturing quality, compliance, and field feedback are connected into a faster domestic learning loop.

Project Goal

Identify where AI can most credibly reduce time-to-capability and what ecosystem enablers India must build alongside it.

Expected Value Created

Tangibles

AI intervention map, ecosystem gap map, sector use-case shortlist, and a build roadmap for data, shared infrastructure, packaging/test, and reliability.

Intangibles

Sharper strategic framing, better decisions on where India should play, and a clearer lens on control versus capacity in electronics.

Why This Problem Is Relevant

01
The bottleneck is structural, not just capacity-driven

India's policy stack itself highlights the missing middle of the ecosystem: semiconductors, components, packaging, and design depth, not just final assembly.

India Semiconductor Mission; MeitY ECMS
02
The timing is unusually favorable right now

China+1 diversification, Apple-supplier expansion, and newly approved semiconductor projects create a real window to convert manufacturing momentum into deeper capability.

Reuters; Bloomberg
03
AI is relevant because it attacks the actual delay points

Published cases already show AI compressing chip design, virtual validation, manufacturing traceability, and yield learning, which are precisely the bottlenecks this project targets.

Google Research; Synopsys; Instrumental
